13/3/24

For the past two weeks, we have been working on a project called an egg drop. The premise of the project is to drop an egg from up high, and trying to protect it so it does not break. Our approach was to load it with as much cushioning and slow fall as possible, and it worked! When dropping the egg, the balloons we attatched to the side was met with resistance from the air, and so the whole contraption was slowed. This made the egg reach the ground at a slower velocity, letting it land safer. Additionally, the large amount of cushioning in the form of cotton balls and crumpled up balls of paper absorbed most of the impact, shieldng the egg. Additionally, if the cushioning did not do enough, we had it so the only contact the egg would make to the ground was through the straw structure surrounding it, allowing for the egg to never take the force of the impact with the ground.
